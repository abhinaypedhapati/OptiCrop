ABOUT THE PROJECT

Smart Agricultural Production Optimization Engine

OptiCrop is a data-driven agritech prototype that leverages machine learning models to help farmers identify which crop is most suitable for their lands' physical and climatic properties.

The Core Problem

Improper crop selection is a primary driver of low crop yield and agricultural bankruptcy. Cultivating crops that are not aligned with soil minerals or regional moisture averages requires excessive chemical fertilizers and water, leading to soil degradation, economic stress, and low environmental sustainability.

Dataset Specifications

The engine is trained on a crop recommendation dataset featuring 2,200 observations across 8 variables:

  • Nitrogen (N): Primary nutrient for plant leaf growth.
  • Phosphorous (P): Vital for root development and cell division.
  • Potassium (K): Regulates water balance and overall plant disease resistance.
  • Temperature: Regional temperature in Celsius scale.
  • Humidity: Atmospheric relative humidity percentage.
  • pH: Soil acidity or alkalinity scale (ideal range: 5.5 to 7.5).
  • Rainfall: Regional annual/seasonal rainfall in millimeters.
  • Label: Target crop class containing 22 unique crop names.

Social & Environmental Impact

By suggesting crops that match local environmental baselines, OptiCrop promotes Precision Agriculture. This reduces excess fertilizer chemical runoff, prevents regional groundwater depletion, improves crop yields, and ensures long-term environmental sustainability.
